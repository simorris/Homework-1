About Mike

I grew up in Bowling Green, a small, farm town in northeast Missouri. I'm a 40 year old father with two boys (ages 8 & 11) and one wife (so far). Amy and I have been together since high school so I'm liking the odds. I've lived in Missouri, Illinois, Indiana, and Iowa. I've been a grocery store carry-out, driven dump trucks and backhoes for the city street department, organized youth sports at a YMCA and summer camps, interned at a municipal recreation department and graduated with a BS in Parks and Recreation Management with some computer courses. After graduation I spent time as an EMT, a short time working as a community support person for people with mental illness, and a very short time (2 weeks) as a furniture salesman.

I was sucked up in the Internet bubble in the late 90s. Bill Gates Foundation gave several million to St. Louis Public Library for technology improvements. I landed a job installing new PCs in the different branches around the city. After a few weeks my job duties expanded to include helping configure WAN links and I was introduced to computer and network security.

After leaving St. Louis I spent time working as a contractor doing phone tech support for McCleod USA sales people. I eventually ended up at Kinze Manufacturing for several years. I worked as network and system administrator and was involved in several major projects and system implementations. After graduation, Amy started teaching at the University of Notre Dame. The first year I was a stay at home dad and then I started teaching as an adjunct in Fall 2005. In 2007 my wife and I landed back at the University of Iowa.
